How It Works

Shopify Margin Engine pipeline: from connect to decision log and outcome tracking. Standardised fee models and repeatable margin checks.

Workflow

1. Connect → 2. Pull orders → 3. Map revenue → 4. Fee model → 5. Cost inputs → 6. Allocate → 7. Compute → 8. Flag risk → 9. Log & track
  1. Authenticate Shopify store — Secure OAuth (conceptually; marketing-level). You connect your store so we can read orders and products.
  2. Pull orders + line items — We fetch orders and line items for the time window you configure.
  3. Map revenue and discounts per line item — Line-level revenue and allocated discounts from Shopify data.
  4. Apply fee models — Shopify fee %, payment fee %, and (if applicable) FX fee %. Configurable per store.
  5. Apply cost inputs — COGS per SKU, packaging default, shipping cost rules. So net margin reflects true cost.
  6. Allocate refunds + ads (Phase 2) — Refund allocation and ad spend allocation per SKU. Method options (e.g. revenue share, equal split) will be clearly explained in-product.
  7. Compute net profit + margin % — Per SKU and per order. You see true net margin, not just revenue.
  8. Flag risk + generate decision prompts — Negative margin, low margin, leakage hotspots. Alerts → decision prompts (reprice, pause ads, change discount, update COGS).
  9. Log decisions and track outcomes — Every recommendation and action is logged. Status: planned → applied → reviewed. Decision workflow with review cadence.

What’s innovative

Cost-risk reduction: Cost allocation and fee modelling that reveal hidden margin leakage. Most merchants don’t see true net margin per SKU once fees, shipping, discounts, and refunds are in the picture. We make that explicit.

Process innovation: System-guided decision workflow—alerts → decision → log → review. Not founder-led heroics; repeatable margin checks and a review cadence so you don’t scale loss-makers and you have an audit trail for pricing and spend decisions.

Avoiding generic “AI” claims: this is a modelling engine + decision workflow, not black-box AI.
