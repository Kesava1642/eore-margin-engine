Use Cases

Four realistic warehouse scenarios: Situation → Decision → Action → Outcome, with metrics.

1. Conveyor stoppage

Situation: Main outbound conveyor stops during peak. Cause unknown.

Decision: What do we do next? Escalate to maintenance or try reset first? Who communicates to shipping?

Action: System recommends: run first 90-minute playbook (triage → notify maintenance + shift manager → redirect flow to secondary line). Shift manager logs the decision and start time.

Outcome: Downtime reduced from 45+ min (ad-hoc) to <25 min. Repeat incidents for same root cause logged and tracked for follow-up.

Metrics: Avg response time −40%; repeat incidents (same asset) −2 in 90 days.

2. Labour shortfall

Situation: Four pickers no-show. Inbound and outbound targets at risk.

Decision: Prioritise inbound or outbound? Reallocate from receiving? When to escalate to ops manager?

Action: System recommends: apply labour reallocation template; escalate if fill rate drops below X% within 30 min. Supervisor logs reallocation and escalation time.

Outcome: Decision time from “we have a shortfall” to “reallocation applied” under 15 min. Fill rate maintained within 5% of target.

Metrics: Decision time <15 min; fill rate variance −5%.

3. Inventory discrepancy (cycle count)

Situation: Cycle count shows large variance in a high-value SKU zone. Possible theft or process error.

Decision: Stop operations in that zone? Escalate to security/ops? Who approves and when?

Action: System recommends: follow discrepancy playbook—quarantine zone for recount, notify ops and (if threshold met) security; log in decision log with approval role.

Outcome: Consistent process across shifts. Audit trail for every discrepancy and approval. Fewer repeat errors due to documented steps.

Metrics: 100% of discrepancies logged; audit-ready in <1 day.

4. Safety incident (near-miss)

Situation: Near-miss reported: forklift and pedestrian in same aisle. No injury but policy requires investigation.

Decision: Who investigates? When to escalate to facilities/safety? What immediate controls (e.g. aisle closure)?

Action: System recommends: first 90-minute safety playbook—secure area, notify safety lead, assign investigator, log in decision log. Escalation if not acknowledged within 15 min.

Outcome: All near-misses logged and tracked. Response time to “investigation started” under 30 min. Repeat incidents by location visible for trend analysis.

Metrics: 100% logged; response time <30 min; repeat incident rate by location tracked.
