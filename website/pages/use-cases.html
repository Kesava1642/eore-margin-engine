Use Cases

Four ecommerce scenarios: Situation → Decision → Action → Outcome. Metrics below are illustrative sample outcomes (e.g. “Stopped spend on 3 loss SKUs, recovered £X/month”)—not claims from live customers unless stated.

1. Scaling a product with high ROAS but negative net margin

Situation: A product has strong ROAS and revenue, so the merchant scales ad spend. After shipping, payment fees, and COGS, net margin is negative.

Decision: Which products are safe to scale? Which need repricing or pause?

Action: EORE surfaces SKU-level net margin. Merchant sees this SKU is loss-making after fees and shipping. Decision: pause ads, reprice or reduce shipping cost, then re-evaluate.

Outcome: Stop scaling loss-makers; reallocate spend to profitable SKUs. Fewer cashflow shocks.

Example metrics (illustrative): Stopped spend on 3 loss SKUs; recovered ~£400/month in margin; decision time from “scale” to “pause” under one week.

2. Discount campaign causing margin collapse on a “top seller”

Situation: A top seller runs on 20% off. Revenue looks great; after discount and fees, margin collapses or goes negative.

Decision: Which products need repricing or discount caps? Where is margin leakage?

Action: Margin Engine allocates discounts per line item and flags low/negative margin. Merchant decides: cap discount for this SKU or raise base price.

Outcome: Price correctly; avoid “revenue-only” scaling on discounted loss-makers.

Example metrics (illustrative): Identified 2 “top sellers” below 5% margin after discount; repriced; margin recovered ~12% on those SKUs.

3. Refund-heavy SKU where refund allocation flips profitability

Situation: A SKU has high refund rate. Without allocating refunds to SKU, it looks profitable; once refunds are allocated (Phase 2), it’s loss-making.

Decision: Which SKUs are actually profitable after returns? Where should we fix product or pause?

Action: (Phase 2) Refund allocation per SKU. System flags “refund-heavy” and suggests: improve product/fulfilment or pause/repricing. Decision logged.

Outcome: True net margin after returns; fewer wrong scaling decisions.

Example metrics (illustrative): 1 SKU flipped from “profitable” to “loss” after refund allocation; paused ads; saved ~£200/month in wasted spend.

4. Multi-currency orders where FX fees erase profit

Situation: Store sells in multiple currencies. FX and payment fees vary; some orders show healthy revenue but net margin is near zero or negative.

Decision: Where is fee leakage? Which products/regions need price or fee assumptions updated?

Action: Fee model includes FX fee %; margin view shows impact per order/SKU. Merchant adjusts pricing or fee assumptions and tracks in decision log.

Outcome: Visibility into true net margin by currency/region; cashflow protection.

Example metrics (illustrative): FX fees identified on 15% of orders; margin impact ~£150/month; pricing rule updated for non-USD.
