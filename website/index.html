EORE — Operational Decision Systems

System-guided decisions at scale. Repeatable process: detect → recommend → decide → track.

The problem

Operational teams lose time and money during disruptions and routine decisions because decision logic is not standardised and actions are inconsistent across shifts. When incidents happen, the wrong priorities get chosen, escalation paths are unclear, and recovery steps vary by who is on duty. The cost of delay—downtime, rework, and repeat incidents—adds up fast.

Who it is for

EORE is built for site-level roles that own operational decisions and execution:

  • Operations Manager — owns day-to-day priorities and escalation
  • Shift Manager — runs first response and handover
  • Warehouse Supervisor — coordinates labour and flow during disruptions
  • Facilities / IT lead — technical recovery and approvals

Decisions we improve

  • What do we do next? — Prioritisation of tasks and incidents
  • When do we escalate? — Clear escalation paths and thresholds
  • What are the recovery steps? — First 90-minute playbooks
  • What is the cost–risk tradeoff? — Visibility into tradeoffs before committing
  • Who approved what? — Decision logs and audit trail

How the system works

Intake → classification → scoring/constraints → recommendation → decision log → review loop. The system surfaces next actions, escalation paths, and templates so every shift follows the same logic. Outcomes are tracked so you can measure response time, repeat incidents, and audit readiness.
